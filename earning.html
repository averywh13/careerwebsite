EARNING POTENTIAL

In May 2021, the median annual wage for an aerospace engineer was $122,270 which is high above the national average ($45,760) for most occupations. In May of 2023, the median annual wage for aerospace engineers increased to $130,720. Depending on the state of where you live can determine your salary. For example, New Jersey’s annual salary would be $101,593 ($48.84 hourly), while Alaska’s annual salary would be $126,957 ($61.04 hourly). Majority of aerospace engineers range between $88,500 (25th percentile) to $132,500 (75th percentile), and the top earners are able to make about $142,500 (90th percentile). In the industry, your position can also determine your wage. As an aerospace system engineer, the annual salary would be about $127,215 ($61.16 hourly), however as a Vice President aerospace engineer, the annual salary would be $215,595 ($103.65 hourly).

The earning potential of aerospace engineers can also be influenced by factors such as experience, specialization, and employer. With increasing years of experience and expertise in specific areas such as avionics, propulsion, or space systems, aerospace engineers may command higher salaries. Additionally, those who pursue advanced degrees or obtain professional certifications in specialized areas can enhance their earning potential further. Employers in the aerospace industry, including aerospace manufacturers, research institutions, government agencies, and defense contractors, often offer competitive salaries and benefits packages to attract and retain top talent.

Moreover, the demand for aerospace engineers is closely tied to technological advancements, industry growth, and government spending on defense and space exploration. As the aerospace sector continues to innovate and expand, the need for skilled engineers is expected to remain strong. This sustained demand, coupled with the specialized nature of aerospace engineering roles, can contribute to a favorable job market and opportunities for career advancement. Overall, aerospace engineering offers the potential for lucrative salaries, especially for those with the right skills, experience, and qualifications in this dynamic and vital industry.
