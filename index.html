Aerospace Engineering

  • OVERVIEW Aerospace engineering is a primary field of engineering that assists in designing, developing, testing, and producing of aircrafts, spacecrafts, and other related systems and equipment Read More.
  • EDUCATION Aerospace engineers require at least a Bachelor's degree to enter the field. Some colleges will offer 5-year programs to allow students to earn a bachelor's and master's degree at the same time. Read More.
  • SKILLS To become a successful aerospace engineer, some of the requirements are a blend of technical expertise, problem-solving skills, and creativity. Read More.
  • EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS Students who graduate from these top programs learn about the science of both air and spacecrafts. Read More.
  • EARNING POTENTIAL Aerospace engineers typically earn competitive salaries, reflecting their specialized skills and expertise. However, actual salaries can vary based on factors such as experience, location, education, and the specific sector of aerospace engineering they work in. Read More.
